Crystal Field Theory — quick reference
In an octahedral complex, d-orbitals split into t₂g (lower, ×3) and eg (upper, ×2). Strong-field ligands force electron pairing (low spin); weak-field allow maximum unpaired electrons (high spin). In tetrahedral complexes the splitting is inverted: e (lower, ×2) and t₂ (upper, ×3), with splitting energy ≈ 4/9 Δoct — so tetrahedral complexes are almost always high spin. CFSE is the stabilisation energy gained by placing electrons in lower-energy orbitals. Magnetic moment μ = √(n(n+2)) BM where n = unpaired electrons.
